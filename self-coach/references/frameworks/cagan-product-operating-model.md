# Cagan — Product Operating Model (TRANSFORMED, 2024)

The global reference model for how product teams should operate. Published in TRANSFORMED (2024), evolution of EMPOWERED (2020).

**Source:** [SVPG — svpg.com](https://www.svpg.com/) | [Product Operating Model — producttalk.org](https://www.producttalk.org/the-product-operating-model/)

## Core: Feature Factory vs. Product Operating Model

| Feature Factory | Product Operating Model |
|---|---|
| Teams receive solutions to implement | Teams receive problems to solve |
| Feature roadmap | Outcome roadmap |
| PM is "mini-CEO" (decides everything) | PM is part of the trio (PM + Design + Eng Lead) |
| Success = delivered on time | Success = solved the problem |
| Discovery is a phase | Discovery is continuous |

## Strategic Hierarchy

```
PRODUCT VISION (3-10 years) — CPO/CEO
    |
PRODUCT STRATEGY (1-3 years) — Head of Product / lead
    |
TEAM OBJECTIVES (quarterly) — Lead <-> Product Teams
    |
PRODUCT DISCOVERY (continuous) — Each PM + Trio
```

**For the leader becoming Head:** the leap is from Team Objectives (defining objectives for teams) to Product Strategy (defining the strategy that informs the objectives) and influence on the Product Vision.

## Product Leadership Theater — Anti-Pattern

> "Leaders who perform empowerment but continue prescribing solutions."

Signs:
- Says "you decide" but reviews and vetoes
- Asks for discovery but defines the expected outcome
- Delegates the "how" but retains the "what" so narrowly that no room is left
- Uses "alignment" as code for "agree with me"

**For the leader:** a strategy-oriented profile sees the fastest path and it's hard to resist the urge to jump in. This is exactly Product Leadership Theater when operating in a leadership role. The test: "do my PMs reach conclusions that surprise me?"

## Coaching Assessment (SVPG)

Cagan defines assessment in 3 categories with dual-rating (importance x ability):

### Knowledge
- Product and technology domain
- User knowledge
- Data fluency
- Business knowledge
- Market/competitor knowledge

### Process Skills
- Discovery (testing techniques, prototyping, experimentation)
- Optimization (growth, analytics, A/B testing)
- Delivery (engineering collaboration, quality, release)

### Individual Skills
- Collaboration
- Stakeholder management
- Evangelism (communicating vision and strategy)
- Leadership

**Free tool:** [svpg.com/coaching-tools-the-assessment](https://www.svpg.com/coaching-tools-the-assessment/)

## 4 Risks for a New Head of Product (Cagan)

1. **Reverting to IC** — "if you're the best PM on the team, you have a problem"
2. **Hiring mini-mes** — profile diversity generates better decisions
3. **Superficial coaching** — giving answers instead of asking questions
4. **Ignoring the culture** — strong product requires a culture of experimentation and honesty
