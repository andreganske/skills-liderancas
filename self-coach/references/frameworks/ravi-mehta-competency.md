# Ravi Mehta — Product Competency Model

12 competencies across 4 pillars. The only published MECE model for PM skills — from APM to VP.

**Source:** [ravi-mehta.com/product-manager-skills](https://www.ravi-mehta.com/product-manager-skills/)
**Free toolkit:** [pages.ravi-mehta.com](https://pages.ravi-mehta.com/)

## The 4 Pillars and 12 Competencies

### Pillar 1: Product Execution
| Competency | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Feature Specification** | Clear specification, acceptance criteria, edge cases |
| **Product Delivery** | Timeline management, dependencies, delivery quality |
| **Product Quality** | Quality standards, bugs, polish, UX consistency |

### Pillar 2: Customer Insight
| Competency | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Fluency with Data** | Define metrics, instrument, analyze, make data-driven decisions |
| **Voice of the Customer** | Research, interviews, feedback loops, deep empathy |
| **UX Design** | Design sense, collaboration with designers, visual quality |

### Pillar 3: Product Strategy
| Competency | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Business Outcome Ownership** | Accountability for business results, not features |
| **Product Vision & Roadmapping** | Long-term vision translated into a coherent roadmap |
| **Strategic Impact** | Influence on the company's direction through product |

### Pillar 4: Influencing People
| Competency | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Stakeholder Management** | Alignment, negotiation, expectation management |
| **Team Leadership** | Motivation, development, team culture |
| **Managing Up** | Managing the relationship with manager/CEO, executive communication |

## Weight by Level — Head of Product

For Head of Product, the competencies with CRITICAL weight:

| Competency | Weight as lead | Weight as Head | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| Business Outcome Ownership | Medium | **Critical** | ↑↑↑ |
| Strategic Impact | Medium | **Critical** | ↑↑↑ |
| Team Leadership | High | **Critical** | ↑ |
| Managing Up | Medium | **Critical** | ↑↑ |
| Product Vision & Roadmapping | High | **Critical** | ↑ |
| Stakeholder Management | High | High | = |

Competencies that DECREASE in weight:
| Competency | Weight as lead | Weight as Head | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feature Specification | Medium | Low | ↓↓ |
| Product Delivery | Medium | Low | ↓↓ |
| Product Quality | Medium | Low | ↓ |

**Ravi Mehta's insight:** even excellent PMs are top in only 3-4 out of 12. The question isn't "am I strong in everything?" — it's "what are my differentiating competencies for the next level?"

## Archetypes (Shape Framework)

Ravi defines 4 PM archetypes by combination of strengths:

| Archetype | Primary strengths | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Builder** | Execution + Customer Insight | Product 0→1, PMF |
| **Tuner** | Execution + Data Fluency | Optimization, growth |
| **Innovator** | Strategy + Customer Insight | Vision, new market |
| **Operator** | Execution + Stakeholder Mgmt | Scale, complexity |

**For the leader (hypothesis based on profile):** likely an Innovator profile (strategy-oriented, curious, empathetic). The gap to Head is that Innovators need to develop Operator skills to scale the org.

## How to Use in Self-Assessment

1. For each competency: rate 1-10 (current ability)
2. For each competency: rate 1-10 (importance for Head at the company)
3. Calculate gap: `importance × (10 - ability)`
4. Sort by gap score
5. Focus on the top 3
