# First 90 Days as Head of Product

Playbook for the first 90 days after promotion. Based on Gibson Biddle, Pragmatic Institute, 3Pillar, and No Fear Product Leadership.

## Why Plan BEFORE the Promotion

Whoever demonstrates they've already thought about the first 90 days signals executive readiness. Use this framework BEFORE asking for the promotion — and present it to the CEO as part of the argument.

## Structure: 30-30-30

### Days 1-30: Listen + Map

**Objective:** understand the actual terrain, not what you think you already know (internal promotion trap).

- [ ] Map the business model in detail (unit economics, real vs. assumed ICP, churn by segment)
- [ ] 1:1 with each C-level member: "What should product be doing for you that it isn't?"
- [ ] 1:1 with Engineering, Design, Sales, CS, and Finance leaders
- [ ] Map product team health (quick assessment of each PM)
- [ ] Document debts: technical, product, organizational
- [ ] Identify 3-5 high-visibility, low-effort "quick wins"
- [ ] Diagnose: feature factory or product operating model?

**Deliverable:** "What I learned in the first 30 days" document — share with CEO.

### Days 31-60: Shared Diagnosis

**Objective:** align your reading with the CEO's before proposing any direction.

- [ ] Present diagnosis to CEO: "Here's what I saw — does it align with your vision?"
- [ ] Product Vision v1 (draft for feedback, not for publication)
- [ ] Accountability Matrix: who decides what in product
- [ ] Proposed cadence: a weekly team sync, an ops meeting, a strategy session
- [ ] Customer personas updated with evidence
- [ ] Stakeholder map + influence plan

**Deliverable:** Shared diagnosis + operating model proposal.

### Days 61-90: First Bets

**Objective:** demonstrate that the Head generates clarity and direction, not more work.

- [ ] Product Vision finalized and communicated to the entire team
- [ ] Roadmap rearticulated in hypothesis + outcome language (not features)
- [ ] Success metrics agreed upon with CEO
- [ ] First explicit strategic decisions (what NOT to do is equally important)
- [ ] Quick wins delivered and communicated in impact language
- [ ] Product team with clarity on priorities and decision criteria

**Deliverable:** Product Strategy v1 + success metrics + first results.

## 8 Pitfalls of the First 90 Days

1. **Applying the previous role's playbook** — "teams are suspicious of cut-and-paste approaches"
2. **Changing everything too fast** — diagnose before prescribing
3. **Continuing to do IC** — the transition signal is when the team sees a behavior change
4. **Ignoring stakeholders** — the first 90 days are about listening, not delivering
5. **Not documenting** — without documentation, nobody sees what you're doing
6. **Being the hero** — solving everything alone instead of building the system
7. **Not having quick wins** — credibility is built with results, not plans
8. **Not aligning with the CEO early** — a misaligned diagnosis becomes conflict later

## For Internal Promotion (the leader's case)

Specific trap: you know the company, the people, and the problems. This creates the illusion that you don't need the 30 days of listening. Wrong.

Internal promotion requires:
- **Listening again as Head** — the answers you get as a lead are different from the ones you get as Head
- **Repositioning relationships** — your peers became your executive peers; your reports need to see the change
- **Not carrying old habits** — what worked as a lead can sabotage you as Head (e.g., hands-on decisions, IC during crises)
