# Executive Presence — Framework for Product Leaders

Based on HBR "The New Rules of Executive Presence" (Jan/2024) + Mironov + Biddle.

## Updated Definition (HBR 2024)

The classic definition of executive presence (gravitas + communication + appearance) has been updated. In 2024:

| Before (up to 2020) | Now (2024+) |
|---|---|
| Gravitas = certainty | Gravitas = **reflection + courage under uncertainty** |
| Communication = eloquence | Communication = **clarity + context + listening** |
| Appearance = physical presence | Appearance = **authenticity + comfort with ambiguity** |

**Executives who projected absolute certainty lost credibility. Those who demonstrated reflection and courage in the face of uncertainty gained it.**

This is especially relevant for a deep-processing profile (if that is one of the traps recorded in `profile.md`): deliberate processing is NOT a weakness in the current executive context — it's a strength, as long as it's externalized.

## Business Impact Language

### What to Replace

| PM Language | Head Language |
|---|---|
| "We're building a new onboarding flow" | "We're launching an experience projected to increase activation by +15% in Q3, impacting ARR by $Xk" |
| "We improved the product" | "We reduced manual intervention by X%, freeing Y hours/month from the operations team, equivalent to $Z/year" |
| "We have 14 initiatives on the roadmap" | "Our portfolio is allocated 60% feature work, 30% growth, 10% scaling — aligned with the product's retention stage" |
| "The SLA improved" | "The SLA hit its target — the threshold for a key partner's approval — protecting $X in recurring revenue" |

### Structure for Executive Briefings (Mironov)

1. **The business problem** — not a feature, not a bug
2. **What the data shows** — metrics in impact language
3. **The recommendation** — what to do and why
4. **The cost of inaction** — urgency in financial terms
5. **What we need** — decision, resource, alignment

### Mironov Model: the lead as "Consigliere"

> "Honest broker who represents product/market/company success, not a specific function."

The Head of Product doesn't defend product against engineering. They defend the company through product. This shifts:
- From "the product team needs" to "the company benefits when"
- From "the roadmap says" to "the data indicates the next $X comes from"
- From "we're behind schedule" to "the opportunity cost of each week of delay is"

## DHM as Executive Language (Biddle)

"How will your product delight customers in hard-to-copy, margin-enhancing ways?"

For the product:
- **Delight:** faster and more accurate decisions → the customer trusts, the end user is treated fairly
- **Hard-to-copy:** a large proprietary usage dataset + behavioral history + a proprietary model
- **Margin-enhancing:** automated decision reduces operational cost; take rate on growing volume

This sentence answers the board in 30 seconds.
