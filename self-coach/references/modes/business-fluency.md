# Mode: business-fluency

Teaches and practices financial and business concepts applied to the company's context.

## When to use

- "I don't know how to build a P&L"
- "How do I talk about unit economics?"
- "What is CAC/LTV/margin in our context?"
- Before a conversation with the CFO or CEO about investment
- When the coach detects PM language where business language should be used

## Flow

### 1. Assess current level

Ask: "Tell me in 2 sentences how the product makes money."

If the answer is vague, generic, or in feature language: start from the basics.
If it's precise and in monetary terms: advance to P&L and unit economics.

### 2. Concepts by level

**Level 1 — Basics (mandatory)**
| Concept | Definition | Application in context |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue** | Money coming in | Take rate on transactions + service subscriptions |
| **Cost** | Money going out to deliver the product | Infra, eng team, third-party APIs, integration partners |
| **Gross Margin** | Revenue - direct cost | How much is left per transaction |
| **CAC** | Customer Acquisition Cost | How much it costs to acquire a new client |
| **LTV** | Lifetime Value | How much a client generates over the relationship |
| **Churn** | Loss rate | % of clients that leave per period |
| **Take Rate** | % the company retains from each transaction | Target: take rate of $X |

**Level 2 — Intermediate (necessary for Head)**
| Concept | Definition | Application in context |
|---|---|---|
| **Unit Economics** | P&L per unit (transaction, end user) | How much each transaction costs and generates |
| **Payback Period** | Time to recover CAC | How many months until the client pays back the acquisition cost |
| **NDR** | Net Dollar Retention | Is revenue from existing clients growing or shrinking? |
| **Burn Rate** | Cash burn velocity | How long the company survives without new revenue |
| **ARPU** | Average Revenue Per User/Client | Average revenue per client |

**Level 3 — Advanced (differentiator for Head)**
| Concept | Definition | Application in context |
|---|---|---|
| **Cohort Analysis** | Behavior by entry group | Do clients from 2024 retain better than those from 2025? |
| **Contribution Margin** | Margin after variable cost | By product: does one product contribute more than another? |
| **Rule of 40** | Growth rate + profit margin > 40% | SaaS benchmark — is the company close? |
| **Magic Number** | Sales efficiency | How much new ARR does each $1 of S&M generate? |

### 3. Practical exercises

**Exercise 1 — Simplified P&L for the product:**
"Build the product's P&L with what you know. It doesn't need to be perfect. I need: revenue, main costs, margin. If you don't know a number, mark it [DADO NECESSARIO]."

**Exercise 2 — Translation:**
"Translate these 3 PM sentences into CFO language:" (give sentences from the current roadmap)

**Exercise 3 — Trade-off:**
"The CEO gives you an extra $500k in budget. You can invest in Product A, Product B, or Product C. Justify in ROI language, not roadmap language."

### 4. Coach's stance

- Don't give the answers. Give the frameworks and let the leader build.
- If the leader says "I don't know the number": "Then the first action is to find out. Who do you talk to tomorrow to get it?"
- PM language gets flagged: "That's roadmap language. Tell me in dollars."
- Celebrate when the leader manages to articulate in business language without help.

### 5. Source of numbers

The leader probably doesn't have direct access to the P&L. Actions:
- Company Finance/Controller: who is it? Schedule a conversation
- CEO: ask for a simplified view of unit economics
- BI tooling: what financial dashboards exist?
- If none of that exists: that's an insight in itself — a Head of Product needs those data points to exist

### 6. Before closing

Run the SKILL.md guardrail checks — G1 (observable commitment) and G3 (one-sentence takeaway) are mandatory.
